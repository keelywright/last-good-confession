April 2026 Elegy

On the Silence That Comes After Naming

There is a room in every house that no one enters anymore. Not because of locks or grief, but because the body simply refuses. The feet do not carry you there. The hands do not reach for that particular door.

"The worst erasures are the ones we perform on ourselves, tenderly, over years."

We think of forgetting as passive — a slow dissolve, like salt in rain. But there is a kind of forgetting that requires maintenance. That must be fed.

March 2026 Fragment

Inventory of What the Fire Did Not Take

One coat. One letter, unfinished. The habit of waking at four. The inability to sleep through a storm. A name you no longer say aloud but still write, sometimes, in fogged glass.

The things we carry are rarely the things we chose.
Most of what survives us survived without permission.

February 2026 Witness

The Ceremony of Small Departures

Every exit is rehearsed. The way you learn to leave a room before the conversation ends. Before the weight of it settles. The body knows the routes — down the back stairs, past the coat rack, into the clean air that asks nothing of you.

We call these escapes. We mean: survival strategies. We mean: the only thing I knew how to do.

"To leave before being left is not cowardice. It is a form of prayer."
